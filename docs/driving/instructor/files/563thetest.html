Test Day

On the day of your test you will need to bring:                     
 -Your photocard driving licence.
 -Appropriate eyewear if you need it.

You should also bring your theory test pass certificate and it's a good idea to bring along written confirmation of your test appointment. These aren’t legal requirements however.

It is not unusual to be nervous on test day. The examiners are very used to nervous candidates and will do their best to put you at ease. However, they are generally quite formal in order to carry out the test to the proper format and so as not to distract you from your driving.

At the test centre, the examiner will meet you in the waiting room and ask you to sign a residency and insurance declaration. They will check your documents and ask if you’d like your instructor to accompany you on the test. This is entirely your choice. If you’d prefer your instructor to wait in the test centre then you’ll be asked if you’d like them present for the debrief at the end of the test. Again, this is your choice.

You will be asked to lead your examiner to the car. On the way, you’ll need to read a number plate from 20.5 metres.

At the car you will be asked one tell me question, either inside or out.  Once inside the car, you should carry out a brief cockpit drill, ready for the drive. After checking around the vehicle, the examiner will join you in the car and explain the test. They may say, for example:

"The test will last between 35 and 40 minutes and include one manoeuvre, twenty minutes of independent driving and maybe an emergency stop. I’d like you to follow the road ahead, unless road signs or markings direct you otherwise, and if I want you to turn left or right i’ll tell you in plenty of time. If you don’t understand any of the directions, ask and i’ll explain again”.

And off you go.

To pass the test you will need to commit no more than fifteen driver faults and no serious or dangerous faults.

Driver fault-Anything which is not totally correct 

Serious fault-A potentially serious mistake which could cause other road users to take action

Dangerous fault-A mistake which causes actual danger to you or other road users

The examiner will discreetly record your test during the drive. Try not to worry if they write, it may not even be a fault they’re recording. If you make a mistake, don’t let it get you down, it may not be as bad as you think. Try to maintain concentration throughout.

At the end of the test you will be asked to relax and remove your seatbelt. The examiner will take a moment to fill in the test report and will then tell you if you’ve passed or not. Either way, they will give you a debrief and explain any significant mistakes they’ve recorded.

If you’ve passed, the examiner will offer to send off your provisional licence and you’ll be given a certificate which will act as a licence until your full licence arrives in the post within three weeks.

If you fail, don’t be disheartened. The national average pass rate is about 45% so statistically most people don’t pass straight away. Try to consider it a valuable learning experience and get yourself even better for next time! 

Good luck!
