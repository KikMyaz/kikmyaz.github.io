All Weather Driving
Snow and Ice


-Consider abandoning the journey if it isn’t essential. Check weather reports.

-If you need to make a journey, especially a long one, consider taking along a spade, old sacks, warm clothing, hot drink, food.

-Remember that there is an increased stopping distance of up to ten times the norm so slow down, increase your distance from the car in front and use extra caution and anticipation.

-When visibility is reduced by falling snow use dipped headlights.

-Heavy snowfall can be very disorientating so use extra caution and slow down.

-Road markings can be covered up so use extra care when working out priorities.

-Snow can affect the braking by gathering around brake linkages under the car. Test them occasionally when safe.

-Use the highest practical gear when not engine braking* and as long as it doesn’t compel a higher speed. This helps with traction.

Braking On Snow/Ice

-All but the gentlest braking can cause a skid.

-Slow down and use extra anticipation to avoid having to brake sharply.

-*Use engine braking where possible. This means changing down gently through the gears so that the lower gear assists with the braking.

-Use a low gear and gentle brake to keep the car moving slowly when travelling downhill.

Cornering On Snow/Ice

-Braking whilst on a bend can easily send the car into a skid so avoid this by slowing sufficiently before reaching the bend.

-Don’t use the clutch on the bend and use the highest practical gear as long as it doesn’t compel you to speed up.

-Steer smoothly around the bend. Sudden steering can cause a skid.

-There is danger of skidding all the way around the bend so maintain care even when coming out of the turn.

Uphill On Snow And Ice

-Use the highest practical gear to reduce the chance of wheelspin but not so high that you lose momentum and have to change on the hill.

-Keep a good gap from the vehicle in front in case they have to stop, you may be able to get round.

Starting On Snow And Ice

-If your wheels have no traction and won’t get the car moving, don’t race the engine as it’ll dig the wheels in further. You can try to gently let the car roll forwards and backwards until traction is gained or use a spade to clear the snow or old sacks to cover it.
