Night Driving
Importance of Lights


-Lights are essential at night to provide information for you and other road users.

-Make sure they’re clean front and rear. Clean windows are important too.

-Make sure all the lights are operational. Check regularly, particularly before and during a long journey. Carry spare bulbs and replace them as soon as possible if they blow. This can be tricky on some models of car. Many car spares outlets will assist you for a small fee.

-Make sure your lights are properly adjusted. Specify this when taking your vehicle in for a service.

When to Use and Why

-Dusk: Don’t be afraid to be the first to put your headlights on. Illuminated streetlights can be a reminder. As daylight fades, visibility can be particularly affected.

-Night time: Lights should be used always when driving at night. They are essential to seeing and being seen.

-Dawn: Leave your lights on until full daylight.

-Daylight: In poor light throughout the day, use dipped headlights. Overcast skies can reduce visibility.

-Weather: In poor light due to rain/sleet/snow, use dipped headlights. In foggy conditions, use foglights. See section below.
