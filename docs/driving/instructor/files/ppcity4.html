Town Driving
Typical Features of Urban Roads


Bus Stops and Lanes

-Anticipate when buses ahead will pull over. Scan up the road for bus stops and any other clues such as passengers on the bus standing up. Take a safe distance behind the bus and prepare to overtake only if safe.

-Be aware that pedestrians can step out from behind buses as they are parked.

-Observe the rules of bus lanes. Stay out of them during their hours of operation and use them otherwise. Keep a keen eye on the blue signs indicating their hours of operation.

 

Trams

-Trams cannot change their course to avoid obstacles so pay attention to signs and road markings which guide you away from their path.

-Diamond shaped signs give instruction to trams only. Where there are no other signs, give way to the tram.

-Be cautious where trams set down and pick up for pedestrians stepping out.

-Take care when crossing tram tracks. They can be slippy so take care when turning and braking on them. Be aware that cyclists can run into trouble on tram tracks as their narrow tyres can get stuck.

-Bear in mind the speed and silent approach of trams.

 

Cycle Paths

-Don’t drive or park in a cycle lane bounded by an unbroken white line.

-Don’t drive or park in a cycle path bounded by a broken white line unless it’s unavoidable.

-Stay back at traffic lights where an area of the road is reserved for cyclists.

-With or without a cycle path, you should allow at least six feet when passing them as they can swerve and wobble around obstructions in the gutter.


Emergency Vehicles

-Look and listen for emergency vehicles. It can be difficult to locate the direction a siren is coming from so be especially alert until you've seen the vehicle.

-If necessary, slow down or pull over, to allow the vehicle to pass, though not before the brow of a hill, bend or narrow stretch of road. Don't be so eager to pull over that you actually block the traffic. Be prepared for other drivers to pull over and continue to comply with all traffic signs.

-Signalling for the emergency vehicle can give its driver extra confidence to pass.
