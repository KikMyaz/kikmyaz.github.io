Sat Nav

Four out of five tests will require you to follow directions from a sat nav for 20 minutes of your driving test. The examiner will pull you over beforehand and explain:

"Shortly i'd like you to drive for some distance independently. I'd like you to follow a series of directions from the sat nav please. Continue to follow the sat nav until I tell you otherwise. Drive on when you're ready".

Location of Device

The examiner will provide the device and locate it safely within your test vehicle. Take note that they will position it near to your line of sight but not obstructive to your view. This will, ideally, be on a special dashmat but due to the design of some vehicles, it may have to be attached to the windscreen.

Programming of device

You won't be expected to program the device on your driving test but bear in mind that outside of test conditions:

  • You should do this only when parked beside the road as your attention could be significantly drawn from the road ahead if attempted whilst on the move.
  • Be very careful that you choose the correct destination. A mistake at this stage could waste you a lot of time. Use a postcode if possible.
  • Generally select the quickest route as opposed to the shortest. This will take you on faster roads designed for volumes of traffic.
  • Turn the volume of the sat nav up so that you can use the verbal commands rather than relying on the on screen instructions which could draw your attention from the road ahead.
Following Directions

Use the verbal prompts from the sat nav to plan your route in good time. 

Glance at the screen if it will help you to understand the route but quickly return your attention to the road ahead. Avoid looking down at the screen when on a bend, tricky junction or roundabout as this could result in a steering or positional error.

Some devices will specify a lane approaching a junction but others will just give you the direction.
