Rural Roads
Sharp Bends


-Respond early to warning signs of sharp bends assessing the situation in terms of:

  • Whether the road has a footpath or if there's a possibility of pedestrians on the road
  • How sharp or dangerous the bend is. The more emphatic the black and white chevron sign is, the sharper the bend. SLOW painted on the road is an indication of a very sharp bend. Look for the line of the hedgerow, trees or streetlights to get an idea of the bends shape.
  • Expect a second bend if the warning sign was for a double bend.

-Always approach at a speed where you could stop safely within the limit of your vision. See Here

-Expect to encounter slow moving horse riders, cyclists, pedestrians or farm vehicles around any corner.

-Get to the appropriate speed and gear approaching the bend, (consider the condition of the road surface), making sure your braking is done before you steer. As you take the bend use the accelerator just enough to keep the vehicle under full control without speeding up. See Here

-Consider using your horn approaching sharp bends on very narrow roads and respond carefully if you get such warning of oncoming vehicles.
