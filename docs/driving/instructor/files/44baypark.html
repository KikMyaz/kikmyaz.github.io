Bay Parking

Reversing Into a Bay

On your test, you may be asked to reverse safely into a parking space in a car park, finishing reasonably centrally within the bay. This will be carried out within the test centre premises.

You will be able to choose your bay on the left or right side.

You should aim to reverse into the bay accurately, first time but don’t worry if you don’t quite manage this. It is acceptable to pull forward and back again, making necessary adjustments. Experienced drivers will often need to do this.

On your driving test, you won’t be tested on the method you use but the safety and control of the manoeuvre.

Illustrated below are two techniques:

Method 1
  1. Call your target bay number 1, then count forward bays 2, 3 and 4. Start with your shoulder just behind the line between 3 and 4, keeping the car straight.
  2. When you’re sure it’s safe, reverse slowly, applying full lock to the left or the right.
  3. Straighten the wheels briskly as the car becomes straight and stop the car when the front end is just within the bay.
Experiment with the starting point if necessary, but practice consistent speed of steering and reverse speed to achieve a consistent finishing position.


Be aware of vehicles parked in the bays around you. Starting from the middle of the channel between the bays, allows a path which doesn’t intrude into another space.

Where there are parked vehicles beside and behind your bay, you can gauge your finishing position by looking around at them. Be especially aware of the vehicle directly behind you to be sure you don’t reverse into it.

Where there are no vehicles beside or behind you, establish a reference to help recognise how far back you can go.

Method 2 
  1. Start with your shoulder just behind the far line of the bay you’re aiming for.
  2. When you’re sure it’s safe, move slowly forward, steering full lock briskly to the right until you reach an angle of about 45 degrees.
  3. Check around again and reverse, applying full lock to the left.
  4. Straighten the wheels when the car is straight and stop when the front end is just within the bay.


Observation

Other vehicles are likely to be manoeuvering in and out of bays around you. Check for occupied vehicles and anticipate their actions.

In a car park you are sharing the road with pedestrians. Keep a keen eye for them walking to and from their vehicles and pause for them to pass where necessary.

Look over your shoulders predominantly when reversing. 

Control

The accuracy and consistency of the manoeuvre depends on the consistency of:
  • Your reference point
  • The speed that you steer
  • The speed that you roll

Steer quickly at the moment you begin to move but be careful not to dry steer (turn the steering wheel whilst the car is stationary) as this can damage the steering column and tyres.

Lower the mirrors if it helps to see the lines but remember to raise them afterwards.

Driving Forwards Into a Bay and Reversing Out

You may be asked to drive forwards into a bay and then reverse out of it. This can be carried out in any car park including that of the test centre.
Parking Forwards
You will not need to park in a bay adjacent to other vehicles.
You won't be allowed to drive through a bay and into the bay in front of it.
  1. Carefully position yourself a few yards away from the side you will steer towards and maintain a straight position (90 degrees with the bay you're aiming for). This will enable you to enter the bay without cutting through the adjacent bays. If you are on a two way road and using the right side, beware of oncoming traffic before you take this position.
  2. Align your mirror with the nearest line of the bay you will drive into. (See illustration below). Experiment with this point if necessary.
  3. Once you've established it's safe, move slowly forwards and immediately steer full lock into bay 3. Beware of vehicles or pedestrians passing before you turn.
  4. Straighten the wheels and stop once straight and within the bay.

Reversing Out Of The Bay
Where the surrounding bays are empty it is acceptable to pass through them when entering and reversing. 
The following technique assumes that the adjacent and surrounding bays are occupied.

1.Make a thorough, all round check especially behind by looking over both shoulders. Make sure there are no pedestrians or vehicles using or about to use the space behind you.

2.Reverse until your shoulders are about level with the end of the bay.

3.Begin by steering just a half turn to the left or right. Steering too much too early could swing the front of the car into the adjacent bay which would be a problem if it were occupied.

4.When the front of the car is about at the end of the bay, add more steering to as much as full lock if possible.

5.Look over your shoulders and if it's safe, reverse until you're close to the opposite bays or far enough out to complete the manoeuvre.

6.Select first gear and after a good, all round check, move forward and steer to the right by as much as full lock if necessary. 

7.Hey Presto!
