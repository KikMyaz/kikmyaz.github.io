Vulnerable Road Users

-Pedesrians cyclists and motorcyclists are more vulnerable than other motorists and due to their narrow profile, they can be much more difficult to see. 

-Unlike other road users, pedal cyclists make no or little road noise.  They can also approach very quickly, especially downhill. When peeping and creeping from a closed junction, your ears might be helping you to judge your progress. Consider the possibility of speedy, silent cyclists.

-At other times you may be approaching and following very slow cyclists. You will probably want to pass. Do You remember how to do this safely?
 
-Always keep in mind the vulnerability of pedestrians. Exercise consideration and patience where they are concerned. 

-Where pedestrians are not using a dedicated crossing, drive according to your priority. However, once a pedestrian is on the road, they assume priority. 

-Old people can be slow when crossing and children can be unpredictable.

-Use speed limits with discretion where there is likely to be pedestrian activity. Remember that at 40mph, your vehicle is likely to kill any pedestrian it hits. At 20mph there is only a one in twenty chance of the pedestrian being killed.
