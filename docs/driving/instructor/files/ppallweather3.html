All Weather Driving
Rain


-If visibility is poor then use dipped headlights.

-Stopping distances can be double in the rain so slow down and increase the gap to the car in front.

-After a dry spell, rain on the road can make the surface even more slippy. Use extra caution.

-Remember that the less tread on your tyres, the greater the stopping distance.

-Go carefully if you have to pass through puddles as they can make you swerve. Try not to soak pedestrians.

-Water can affect the effectiveness of the brakes. Test the brakes when it is safe. This will also help to dry them off.

-Be careful when overtaking and approaching heavy vehicles as the spray they produce can seriously impair your visibility. Use the fastest setting of your wipers.

Aquaplaning

-In heavy rain and especially at higher speeds, the tyres can become separated from the road by a thin film of water. Even good tyres can’t grip in this situation.

-You might tell that you’re aquaplaning if your steering suddenly becomes very light.

-If this happens, just gently ease off the accelerator until control is returned. Don’t brake or steer as this will have no effect.

Floods

-As you approach a flooded road, consider stopping to assess the depth. As a measure, avoid travelling through where the water level will be higher than half a wheel.

-Remember that, due to the camber of the road, the water will be shallowest at the centre. If the water is too deep then turn around and take another route.

-Deep water can get into the exhaust system and cause the engine to stop or get into the air intake on some vehicles and cause considerable damage. It can also affect the electrical components and engine management system.

-If the water is not too deep then drive through slowly in first gear. Try to avoid stalling by slipping the clutch and keeping the engine speed high.

-Once through the flood test your brakes. You may need to dry them out by driving along slowly whilst applying gentle brake pressure.

-Never try to displace the water by driving at it fast. You will probably stall, could lose control and may damage the car.

-Never drive through fast flowing water. More than half of deaths in floods are in vehicles which are swept away.
