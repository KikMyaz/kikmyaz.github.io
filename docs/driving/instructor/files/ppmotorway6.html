Motorway Driving
Breakdowns on the Motorway


-If you suffer mechanical failure on the motorway and cannot reach the next services safely then pull onto the hard shoulder as far left as you can, away from the traffic. Have your wheels to the left so that if you were hit from behind your vehicle wouldn’t get pushed into the traffic.

-Put on your hazard lights. Use your sidelights in poor visibility.

-Don’t open the offside doors

-Warn your passengers of the dangers and exit everyone from the vehicle via the nearside doors.

-Keep animals inside the vehicle.

-Ask your passengers to wait on the embankment near the vehicle but away from the traffic.

-Look for a telephone symbol on a marker on the hard shoulder. They are situated every 100 metres. Walk in the direction the arrow indicates to the emergency telephone which will put you in direct contact with the police or the Highway Agency Traffic Officers. Keep to the inside of the hard shoulder as you wait.

-Walk back and wait with your passengers for help.

-If you’re travelling alone and feel threatened then wait in your car with the doors locked. If anybody suspicious approaches, speak to them through a small gap in the window. Tell them the emergency services are on the way.

-Never cross the carriageway or a slip road for any reason.

-Never attempt even simple repairs on the carriageway or hard shoulder.

-Never place any warning device on the carriageway or hard shoulder. Gusts could blow it into the traffic.
