Rural Roads
Gradients


On undulating roads, beware of  ‘dead ground’, that is a dip in the road which might hide an oncoming vehicle.

Where there are steep hills, make sure you have full control of the car.

Uphill

-Look for warning signs telling you how steep the slope is.

-Consider using a lower gear. If you need to change on the hill, change quickly but accurately as you’ll lose momentum as soon as the clutch is pressed.

-Overtaking will be much more difficult uphill and oncoming vehicles could have trouble slowing so only consider if really necessary.

-Keep a distance from the vehicle in front in case they have difficulties.

Downhill

-Look for warning signs telling you how steep the slope is.

- Combined with careful use of the footbrake, a lower gear will help control your speed providing a little ‘engine braking’.

-Be sure to use a lower gear on long downhill stretches as excessive braking on hills can result in ‘brake fade’ where the heat of the friction causes a loss of braking effect.

-Don’t overtake down a steep hill unless you can see it to be clear a long way ahead. The vehicle you’re overtaking could accelerate quickly too.

-Keep a safe distance from the vehicle ahead as you will find it difficult to stop quickly.
