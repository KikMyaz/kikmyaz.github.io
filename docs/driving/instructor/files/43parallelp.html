Parallel Park

You will need to be able to reverse into a space behind a parked car, finishing reasonably close to the kerb and leaving no more than a car length between.

This should be carried out at a safe, legal and convenient location so areas where there are parking restrictions, busy traffic or poor visibility due to bends or hills should be avoided.

Below is one method of carrying out this manoeuvre. Your instructor may teach a different technique or a variation on this one. On your test, you will not be judged on the exact method but on the safety and control of the manoeuvre. 



It is useful to establish a reference point and  the nearside mirror can be used to do this. You may need to lower the mirror but be sure to adjust it by just the same amount each time for a consistent reference.

When you have reached the optimum point of turn, (Your instructor can help you the first few times), try remembering where the kerb meets the side of the car. 
More specifically, focus on the bottom edge of the kerb. This line will remain a consistent reference point whether it is a high or a low kerb.

Stop here and make an all round check before steering right.

Don’t forget to reset your mirror when you’re finished.


Dealing With Other Traffic

Try to begin the manoeuvre when there are no approaching vehicles but you can expect to encounter them as you proceed. 

Thorough observations will ensure you’re aware of traffic in which case pause, if need be, allowing it to pass.

Be particularly careful before you apply the first turn as the front of your vehicle will swing out into the road. Don't force traffic to stop as this happens. 

Do not be overly hesitant when cars are waiting. This doesn't mean you should feel pressurised and rush but be efficient and careful to complete the manoeuvre promptly, safely and accurately.

As you reverse, check directly over both shoulders. Just using the mirrors can give a limited and confusing perspective. 

Don't reverse directly towards pedestrians. Allow them peace of mind by pausing for the few metres it takes them to pass the back of the car.
