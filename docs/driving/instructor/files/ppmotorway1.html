Motorway Driving
Preparing your Vehicle to Drive on a Motorway


High speeds and long distances increase the chance of mechanical failure so carry out the following checks on your vehicle beforehand:

-Tyres: Make sure they’re in good condition and inflated to the correct pressure for motorway driving. This is indicated in the vehicle handbook.

-Instruments: Make sure there are no faults.

-Mirrors: Make sure they’re clean and correctly positioned

-Windscreen and windows: Make sure they’re clean and that the screen wash reservoir is full.

-Lights and indicators: Make sure they’re all working correctly.

-Fuel: Make sure you have enough fuel so you don’t run out between service areas.

-Oil: Check that the oil level is correct before and after a long journey. Vehicles burn more oil at high speeds.

-Water: Check that the engine coolant level is correct. Higher speeds mean higher engine temperature.

Brakes: Check your brakes before setting off.

Steering: Make sure your steering is in good order

Load: Make sure that any load you’re carrying is secure.


Driver Preparations for Driving On a Motorway

-You should have a thorough understanding of the section of the Highway Code dealing with motorways

-You should be familiar with motorway signs and signals.

-You should be fit and alert. Never set out on a journey if you’re unwell or tired, especially on a motorway.


Journey Planning

-Plan your route beforehand with a map or sat nav.

-Estimate journey time and leave enough time so that you won't be late if this is a factor.

-Allow for regular breaks and refreshments.

-Be prepared for route disruptions and traffic. Consider alternative routes and pull over at services to re set sat navs or refer to maps when re planning.
